Neurological Diseases

Multiple Sclerosis

MS

Multiple Sclerosis (MS) disease is a chronic nervous system disease that shows its effect in the central nervous system and manifests itself with attacks.

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis

ALS

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, or ALS, is a rare group of neurological diseases that result from damage to nerve cells primarily responsible for controlling voluntary muscle movement.

Spinocerebellar ataxia

SCA

Spino cerebellar ataxia (SCA) is a disease in which imbalance occurs as a result of loss of balance cells in the cerebellum and spinal cord or inability to function properly.

Alzheimer's

Dementia

Alzheimer's Alzheimer's disease is a medical condition that develops in the form of memory loss, dementia (dementia) and a general decrease in cognitive functions due to the death of brain cells over time.

Myelitis is a name given to inflammation of the spinal cord. It is a disease that affects the neck or back region of the spinal cord.
